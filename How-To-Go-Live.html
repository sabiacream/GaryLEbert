Set Up the Contact Form (5 min)

So estimate requests get emailed directly to Gary ‚Äî free forever

  1. 1
    Go to formspree.io and click "Get Started Free" Sign up with any email address. The free plan allows 50 submissions/month ‚Äî more than enough.
  2. 2
    Click "+ New Form" Name it something like "Gary Ebert Estimate Request". Set the email to Gary's email address.
  3. 3
    Copy your Form ID ‚Äî it looks like: xpzgkbvd It's in the form's endpoint URL: formspree.io/f/xpzgkbvd
  4. 4
    Open index.html in a text editor (Notepad works fine) Press Ctrl+H (Find & Replace). Find: YOUR_FORMSPREE_ID ‚Äî Replace with your actual ID (e.g. xpzgkbvd). Click Replace All. Save.
  5. 5
    Verify your email address Formspree will send a confirmation email. Click the link in it to activate your form.
‚úÖ That's it for the form. Once deployed, every estimate request will land directly in Gary's inbox with the client's name, phone, service type, and project details.

Adding Your Real Photos

Replace the placeholder boxes with Gary's actual project photos

Right now the portfolio and about sections have colored placeholder boxes. Here's how to swap in real photos:

  1. 1
    Create an images folder inside your Gary folder Put all your photos in there. Name them simply: about-gary.jpg, portfolio-1.jpg, portfolio-2.jpg, etc.
  2. 2
    Open index.html in Notepad (or any text editor) Press Ctrl+F to search for about-img-placeholder.
  3. 3
    Replace the placeholder div with an <img> tag Find the block that starts with <div class="about-img-placeholder"> and replace the whole div with:
    <img src="images/about-gary.jpg" alt="Gary L. Ebert" style="width:100%;height:420px;object-fit:cover;">
  4. 4
    For portfolio photos, find each <div class="portfolio-placeholder"> Replace with: <img src="images/portfolio-1.jpg" alt="Project photo" class="portfolio-img">
    The CSS class portfolio-img already handles sizing and hover zoom effects.
üí° Photo tip: Resize photos to max 1200px wide before uploading (use free tool at squoosh.app). This keeps the site loading fast.
